Regional guide

Tourist sites by region

A practical guide to Ghana's 16 regions for travellers who want to build a route around heritage, nature, food, festivals, wildlife, and everyday local life.

How to use this guide

Choose a region, then shape the route

This is a curated planning guide rather than a claim that every local attraction is listed. Use it to decide where to start, then Local Insights can build a private route around your time, pace, and interests.

Region

Ahafo

A forest-belt region known for cocoa, cashew, birding, rocky landscapes, and quieter nature-focused experiences around Goaso and Mim.

Tour sites

  • Mim Buo at Mim
  • Mim Lake
  • Asumura White-Necked Rockfowl Conservation
  • Okomfo Anokye tree at Sankore

Region

Ashanti

The cultural heartland of Asante heritage, royal history, markets, craft villages, lakeside scenery, and rainforest reserves.

Tour sites

  • Manhyia Palace Museum
  • Okomfo Anokye Sword Site
  • Kejetia Market
  • Bonwire Kente Weaving Village
  • Ntonso Adinkra Craft Village
  • Lake Bosomtwe
  • Bobiri Forest Reserve
  • Owabi Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Bomfobiri Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Kumasi Fort and Military Museum

Region

Bono

A green middle-belt region with waterfalls, wildlife, ancient settlement history, river scenery, and relaxed cultural stops around Sunyani and Wenchi.

Tour sites

  • Bui National Park
  • Bui Dam
  • Nchiraa Waterfalls
  • Wurobo Ancestral Caves
  • Duasidan Monkey Sanctuary
  • Hani Archaeological Site

Region

Bono East

A heritage and nature corridor with waterfalls, sacred groves, caves, wildlife, and important sites connected to old Bono history.

Tour sites

  • Kintampo Waterfalls
  • Fuller Falls
  • Buabeng-Fiema Monkey Sanctuary
  • Buoyem Caves and Bats Colony
  • Bono Manso Slave Center
  • Tano Boase Sacred Grove
  • Digya National Park

Region

Central

Ghana's best-known heritage coast, balancing castles, coastal towns, rainforest adventure, festivals, craft villages, and reflective return journeys.

Tour sites

  • Cape Coast Castle
  • Elmina Castle
  • Fort St. Jago
  • Kakum National Park
  • Assin Manso Slave River
  • Assin Praso
  • Ajumako Craft Village
  • Winneba
  • Fort Amsterdam
  • Brenu Akyinim Beach

Region

Eastern

A scenic region of gardens, waterfalls, ridges, lake routes, cocoa history, forest reserves, and easy nature escapes from Accra.

Tour sites

  • Boti Falls
  • Akaa Falls
  • Umbrella Rock
  • Aburi Botanical Gardens
  • Bunso Arboretum
  • The Big Tree
  • Akosombo Dam
  • Lake Volta and Dodi Island
  • Koforidua Beads Market
  • Tetteh Quarshie Cocoa Farm

Region

Greater Accra

The capital region blends independence landmarks, old coastal neighbourhoods, museums, markets, arts, nightlife, beaches, and wildlife reserves.

Tour sites

  • Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park
  • Independence Square and Black Star Gate
  • National Museum
  • Osu Castle
  • Jamestown Lighthouse and Community
  • Makola Market
  • National Theatre
  • Legon Botanical Gardens
  • Shai Hills Resource Reserve
  • Ada Foah
  • Bojo Beach
  • Kokrobite Beach

Region

North East

A rugged northern region shaped by Mamprugu history, escarpment views, rock art, ancient walls, sacred architecture, and river landscapes.

Tour sites

  • Gambaga Escarpment
  • White Volta and Red Volta Rivers
  • NaYiri Palace in Nalerigu
  • NaJeringa Historic Slave Wall
  • Ancient rock art at Gingana, Kpatiritinga, Jilik, and Tusugu
  • Zayaa Mosque-Shrine in Wulugu
  • Buyuori Cave
  • Ancient Koma Figurines in Yikpabongo

Region

Northern

A broad savanna region centred on Tamale, traditional architecture, craft production, Dagbon history, festivals, and routes toward northern heritage sites.

Tour sites

  • Greenwich Meridian Point in Yendi
  • Tamale Zoological Garden
  • Tamale Craft Centre
  • Kukuo Pottery Centre
  • Gushugu Local Textile Centre
  • Yendi heritage sites
  • Sacred Grove and Jaagbo Shrine near Tamale
  • Larabanga Mosque route extension

Region

Oti

A lakeside and savanna region with mountain views, forest reserves, river communities, festivals, and less crowded nature routes.

Tour sites

  • Kyabobo National Park
  • Lake Volta
  • Breast Mountain
  • Chilinga
  • Chaiso Forest Reserve
  • Hanging Village of Shiare
  • Dambai and Oti River routes

Region

Savannah

A major wildlife and heritage region known for Mole, ancient Islamic architecture, eco-villages, slave-route history, and river ports.

Tour sites

  • Mole National Park
  • Larabanga Mosque
  • Larabanga Mystic Stone
  • Mognori Eco Village
  • Kulmasa Crocodile Pond
  • Sonyo Eco-Village
  • Salaga Slave Well and Market
  • Daboya Waterfall
  • Makango Water Safari
  • Yepei and Buipe ports

Region

Upper East

A northern gateway region with sacred crocodiles, hill shrines, pottery, basketry, historical sites, markets, and strong craft traditions.

Tour sites

  • Paga Crocodile Pond
  • Tongo-Tenzug Shrine
  • Sirigu Art and Pottery Village
  • Bolgatanga Market
  • Bolgatanga Museum
  • Navrongo Cathedral
  • Paga-Nania Slave Camp
  • Kulungugu Bomb Site

Region

Upper West

A quieter northern region with hippo conservation, ancient mosques, Sudanese-style architecture, craft traditions, caves, and slave-raid history.

Tour sites

  • Wechiau Hippo Sanctuary
  • Gwollu Slave Defence Wall
  • Nakore Ancient Mosque
  • Wa Naa's Palace
  • Gbelle Game Reserve
  • Dondoli Mosque
  • Bulenga Caves
  • Sankana Caves

Region

Volta

A lush region of waterfalls, mountain villages, monkey sanctuaries, weaving communities, lake scenery, and strong Ewe cultural traditions.

Tour sites

  • Wli Falls
  • Amedzofe Village
  • Tagbo Falls
  • Mount Afadja
  • Tafi Atome Monkey Sanctuary
  • Kpetoe Kente Weaving Village
  • Ho and Asogli cultural routes
  • Volta Lake and Adome Bridge route extensions

Region

Western

A coastal and forest region with beaches, surf towns, forts, wetlands, rainforest reserves, fishing communities, and Nzema-Ahanta culture.

Tour sites

  • Ankasa Nature Reserve
  • Bia National Park route extension
  • Busua Beach
  • Princess Town Beach
  • Fort Metal Cross at Dixcove
  • Fort Batenstein at Butre
  • Axim and Fort Santo Antonio
  • Nzulezu stilt village route extension

Region

Western North

A forested cocoa and timber region with rivers, hilly landscapes, wildlife habitat, and emerging nature-based experiences around Wiawso.

Tour sites

  • Bia National Park
  • Bia Biosphere Reserve
  • Bonsai Waterfalls
  • Mystery Hole
  • Ankobra River route stops
  • Bia River landscapes
